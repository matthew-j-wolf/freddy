The plan to help the Colorado River is to have as many people as possible reduce and reuse the most amount of water we can. We should use as little water as we can. You should always know where your water comes from, because chances are that some, or all of your water comes from the Colorado River. A list of ways to reduce are:

  1. Shorten showers or taking baths
  2. Install water-saving shower heads or flow restrictors
  3. Turning off water while brushing teeth, shaving, rinsing dishes, or cleaning veggies
  4. Running clothes washers and dishwashers only when they are full
  5. Fixing leaks
  6. Not using toilets as an ashtray or wastebasket
  7. Putting a plastic bottle in toilet tanks
  8. Using native and drought-tolerant plants
  9. Choosing food wisely
  10. Keeping a bottle of drinking water in the refrigerator
  11. Watering lawns only when it needs water
  12. Do not play with hoses or sprinklers
  13. And use brooms to clean instead of water
Even following half of these can save almost 1,000 gallons of water a month. However, that's not Another part of saving the Colorado is reusing water. We can reuse by:
  1. using water reuse systems and water-efficient homes and businesses
  2. treating water that has been used in homes and businesses
  3. blend water with other water supplies
  4. use water from bathroom sinks, showers, bathtubs, and washing machines and then using it again in the same home

A few ways to integrate planning efforts are sharing planning information and data between places, providing input and output about long-term plans, and using codes, regulations, or fee structures to promote water efficiency, using different water supplies, and more.

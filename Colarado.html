COLORADO RIVER
By Frederick Wolf

The Colorado river is in danger. 40 million people rely on it for their water. And if it dries up, then 40 million people will be in big trouble.

To get started, here are some facts about it:

  • The average depth of the river is 20 feet
  • The Colorado is 1,450 miles long
  • The Colorado River flows through seven states in the United States
  • The Colorado river is the 46th longest river in the world
  • The Colorado is the sixth longest river in the nation
  • The river has lost about 20 percent of its size over the last century
  • The river starts at La Poudre Pass Lake and ends in the Gulf of California
  • The Colorado irrigates almost 5 million acres of land
  • It is also a water source for 30 tribes
  • Nearly 70% of Colorado River water use is for agriculture
  • The River basin is divided into an Upper and Lower Basin
  • Nearly 90% of Colorado River water originates in the Upper Basin.
  • The Colorado River has been running for six million years
